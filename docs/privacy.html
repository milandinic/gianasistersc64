Privacy Policy — Giana Sisters C64

Last updated: 14 June 2026

This privacy policy explains how the mobile game Giana Sisters C64 ("the app", "we") handles your information. The app is a free, single-player platformer clone with an optional online high-score leaderboard.

1. Information we collect

The app does not require an account and does not collect personal identifiers such as your real name, email address, phone number, precise location, contacts, or any advertising / device identifier.

The only information that ever leaves your device is the data you choose to submit to the online leaderboard:

  • The name (nickname) you type when you achieve a high score;
  • Your score and the level you reached;
  • A timestamp of when the score was achieved.

If you never submit a high score, no data is transmitted from the app.

2. How your information is used

The submitted name, score and level are stored solely to build and display the public high-score leaderboards (an all-time list and a daily list) shown inside the app. We do not use this data for advertising, profiling, or any other purpose.

3. Public visibility of your nickname

The nickname you enter for a high score is shown publicly to other players on the leaderboard. Please do not enter your real name or any personal information as your nickname — choose a non-identifying nickname instead.

4. Data stored on your device

The app stores game settings (such as sound on/off) and a cached copy of the most recent leaderboard locally on your device so the game works offline. This information stays on your device and is removed when you uninstall the app.

5. Third-party services

Leaderboard data is stored using Supabase, a hosted database and functions provider, which acts as our data processor. Network requests to Supabase transmit only the leaderboard data described in Section 1. The app contains no advertising SDKs and no analytics SDKs.

6. Data retention

Leaderboard entries are kept for as long as the leaderboard service operates. You may request removal of a leaderboard entry by contacting us at the address below; because entries are not tied to an account, please include the nickname, score and approximate date so we can identify the entry.

7. Children

The app is intended for a general audience and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because nicknames are entered freely by users, we ask all players to avoid entering personal information.

8. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete information relating to you. Since the app collects no account or contact data, the only such information is a leaderboard nickname you chose to submit. Contact us to exercise these rights.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

10. Contact
